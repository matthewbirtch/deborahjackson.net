Interview in the Ottawa Sun

Not everyone gets a second chance. And not all of us grab the opportunity when it presents itself.

But Ottawa author Deborah Jackson made the most of hers.

When Jackson penned her first novel, there was no such thing as e-books. To get published required a lot of work. Find an editor, find an agent, maybe, find a publisher. After you jump through all those necessary hoops, you hope the book sells.

Jackon’s Ice Tomb did sell. Enough to convince her that she had a career in writing even if the money coming in suggested otherwise. But thanks to e-books, Ice Tomb has a second life and it’s convinced Jackson that e-books are a necessary tool in the writer’s toolkit.
