The Sad Story of Floyd Collins

An Excerpt from Sinkhole

Visions swam around her as she lay in the rock stenosis, barely able to breathe. Images of Floyd Collins, the famous Kentucky caver of 1925. The man wasn’t famous for his caving prowess, which was exceptional, but the manner of his death. In tiny, unspectacular Sand Cave, Floyd was looking for a link to the longest cave system in the world—Mammoth Cave—when a 27-pound rock fell on his foot in a tight vertical passage and trapped him. He couldn’t reach down to loosen the rock and, when friends found him, they couldn’t get around the tight squeeze to help him, nor could they pull him up. As the earth shifted, even the minor aid of food and heat they were supplying him with was cut off. Engineers sank a 55-foot shaft into the earth, but still couldn’t reach him. His peril became a tourist attraction, and as reporters and gawkers milled around above the cave for two weeks, Floyd finally died.

I visited Sand Cave, along with the fabulous Mammoth Cave System, this past summer. To learn more about the longest cave system in the world, and our adventures, see my blog.
